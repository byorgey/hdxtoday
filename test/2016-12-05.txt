<h2>Pickering Foreign Affairs Fellowship Program<o:p></o:p></h2>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing"><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing">The Thomas R. Pickering Fellowship Program is designed to attract outstanding college students from all ethnic, racial and social backgrounds who demonstrate strong leadership and academic excellence, possess a passion for public service,
 and have an interest in pursuing a foreign service career with the U.S. Department of State. This competitive fellowship provides undergraduate and graduate students with financial support (up to $37,500 annually), mentoring and professional development to
 prepare them academically and professionally for this rewarding career path. Women, members of minority groups historically underrepresented in the foreign service, and students with financial need are encouraged to apply. More information is available
<a href="http://www.twc.edu/pickering">online</a>. The application deadline is January 13, 2017. Interested students should begin work on their application as soon as possible to ensure all required components are submitted and received well before this deadline.
 Email <a href="mailto:gess@hendrix.edu">gess@hendrix.edu</a> to arrange an appointment to begin this process.<o:p></o:p></p>

<h2>Odyssey Trip: Peace, War, and memory in Japan and Korea<o:p></o:p></h2>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing"><o:p>&nbsp;</o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNoSpacing">This spring May 11 &#8212; May 25, Dr. Michael Sprunger will lead a GA Odyssey trip exploring the significance of collective and individual memories of war and peace in contemporary Japan and South Korea. In addition to touring and interrogating
 commemorative sites, participants will have opportunities to engage public officials, activists, participants/survivors and peers about the ongoing significance of World War II and the Korean War to current politics and everyday life in and between the two
 countries. If you are interested in applying for the trip, please come to an information session with Dr. Sprunger Monday, Dec. 5, at 4:10 p.m. in Mills C. Contact
<a href="mailto:sprunger@hendrix.edu">sprunger@hendrix.edu</a> with any questions.<o:p></o:p></p>
